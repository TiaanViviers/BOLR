## Modelling implications

- Target construction: label-3 sparsity should be judged against `days_without_label_3` and the sensitivity table. If too many days lose label-3, a softer top-tail or wider positive floor is safer.
- Configuration representation: neighbour roughness and plateau sizes indicate whether a structured 2D grid representation is justified instead of treating configs as unrelated IDs.
- Smoothing: large plateau fractions and high identical-neighbour rates support explicit smoothing priors or local sharing across adjacent configs.
- Adaptation: the day-to-day movement and top-set overlap metrics quantify how aggressively an online model must update to track regime changes.

## Remaining open questions

- Whether near-identical PnL should be collapsed into tolerance-aware labels rather than strict ranks.
- Whether tie handling for top regions should expand to include all tied configs instead of fixed-size deterministic sets.
- Whether online adaptation should target the best point estimate or a broader connected high-PnL region.
